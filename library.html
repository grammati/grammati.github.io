Library

I do almost all my reading on Kindle, so I don't have a bookshelf to gaze fondly upon. So instead, this page is my virtual bookshelf.

I list only books I have actually finished, or am currently reading. They are in reverse chronological order, with the most recently completed at the top.

Stone Mattress

Margaret Atwood

Completed: Feb

Keeping this reading log, I'm realizing that I have been reading a lot of short story collections recently. Anyway, this one was good. The common theme is that the stories are all about elderly people.

The Lost Girls of Camp Forevermore

Kim Fu

Completed: Feb

Having been blown away by Kim Fu's short story collection, I couldn't wait to read this novel. It did not disappoint. Briliantly written. Fu's writing style is incredible; I kept finding myself stopping to re-read a sentence because I was just so struck by it. It's a rare author that has that effect.

How the World Really Works

Vaclav Smil

Completed: Feb

A great overview of how all of human society functions. It covers how we get our energy, our food, transportation. And everything is quantitative. Smil uses hard numbers to argue for what kind of society-wide changes (eg: to slow climate change) are at all feasible, and which are pure fantasy.

Influx

Danial Suarez

Completed: Feb

A sufficiently entertaining, pulpy fictiony sci-fi thingy. Kind of silly; you have to suspend a lot of disbelief, but otherwise, it was OK.

The Ascent of Money

Niall Ferguson

Completed: Feb

This is the first book I have ever "read" by listening to it.

Viruses: A Very Short Introduction

Dorothy H. Crawford

Completed: Jan

A broad but fairly shallow overview of virusues. Types, effects of infection, etc.

Atonement

Completed: Jan

Utterly brilliant. A fantastic an moving novel.

Lesser Known Monsters of the 21st Century

Kim Fu

Completed: Jan

Wow.

I don't even remember how I stumbled onto this book, but I'm so glad I did! This is Ted Chiang-caliber of story writing. Brilliant. Easily one of the best short story collections I have read. You have to read this.

The Invisible Kingdom

Meghan O'Rourke

Completed: Jan

An account of the author's years-long struggle with chronic disease, and dealing with a medical system that struggles to help. Really good.

Men Without Women

Haruki Murakami

Completed: Jan

Murakami is always just so, so good. This book is no exception.

Foundation

Isaac Asimov

Completed: Jan

I read this as a teenager, but remembered very little about it. For a book that's considered such a classic, it was kind-of meh for me, story-wise.

More interesting was that the book highlighted how hard predictions are (especially about the future). Asimov was writing about a time thousands of years away, but some of the technology described seems quaintly anachronistic, even by today's standards, mere decades after writing. For example, a character gets off a spaceship and gets into a taxi driven (or flown) by a human driver. Another asks for a "copy" of a book. And the overarching concept, that a man can can predict the future through the science of psychology is laughable now - these days psychology is barely considered a science at all.
