I Am a Zsh Ninja


OK, not really. Not even close.

But like a lot of programmers, I get ridiculously obsessed with making things work exactly the way I want them to. This morning I wanted to put all the jars in a certain folder on my CLASSPATH. Unfortunately, my java (openJDK) doesn't seem to recognize wildcards in the classpath - eg: "/whatever/*.jar" does not work. So I thought "no problem - with a bit of commandline-fu, I can expand that wildcard". Except I have no commandline-fu. Nevertheless, I found myself compelled to figure out how to do it, ignoring the 500 other, more productive things I could be doing. 40 minutes later:

export CLASSPATH=$(echo ~/.clojure/lib/dev/*.jar | ruby -e "puts STDIN.read.gsub(/ /,':')")

Yippee! I'm sure there's a shorter way to do that, and I'm sure a lot people could have done it in about 3 seconds, but for me it's a victory.

By the way, I don't recommend trying to learn awk by typing "man awk" - trust me, I tried that, and I got nowhere.
